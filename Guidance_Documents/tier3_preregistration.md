# Tier-3 Human Pairwise Preference Study: Pre-Registration

**Status:** Pre-registered; IRB review pending. Run deferred to post-submission.
**Registered by:** Patrick Cooper
**Date of pre-registration:** 2026-05-10
**Cited in paper:** ACL_paper.tex -- Conclusion and Limitations sections.

---

## 1. Research Question

Does Narrative Chain-of-Thought (N-CoT) scaffolding produce responses to
ethical dilemmas that human raters with graduate-level ethical training prefer
over responses produced by Standard Chain-of-Thought (Standard CoT) or
Baseline I/O prompting, when assessed in a blinded pairwise design?

---

## 2. Pre-Registered Hypotheses

**H1 (primary):** Human raters prefer N-CoT responses over Standard CoT
responses at a rate significantly greater than 0.50 (one-sided Wilcoxon
signed-rank test, alpha = 0.05).

**H2 (secondary):** The preference advantage for N-CoT over Standard CoT is
consistent in direction across all five generator models (directional test;
no correction for multiple comparisons; treated as exploratory).

**H3 (secondary):** N-CoT responses receive higher ratings on the sub-dimension
"quality of stakeholder representation" compared to Standard CoT responses.

**Null-result interpretation:** If H1 is not supported, the result is
interpreted as "N-CoT structural effects are not perceptible to trained human
raters at N=50 pairs." This outcome is reportable and does not invalidate
Experiments 1--2, which are measured on structural/behavioural outcomes rather
than rater preference.

---

## 3. Study Design

### 3.1 Sample size

Fifty (50) pairwise comparisons, balanced by topic group (10 topics x 5 pairs
per topic) and generator (10 pairs per generator, sampled from the Phase 1
DailyDilemmas pool).

Power calculation: at N=50 pairs, a one-sided Wilcoxon signed-rank test has
approximately 80% power to detect a preference advantage with effect size
r = 0.30 (medium) at alpha = 0.05.

### 3.2 Stimuli

From the Phase 1 DailyDilemmas 100-scenario pool, 50 scenarios are sampled
deterministically (seed=99) such that:
- Each of the 10 DailyDilemmas topic groups contributes exactly 5 scenarios.
- Each of the five generator models contributes exactly 10 pairs (one scenario
  in each pair is generated by that model under narrative_cot, the other under
  standard_cot; generator identity is always the same within a pair).
- Pairs are constructed by selecting the sample_idx=0 output for both conditions
  from the Phase 1 cache (N=1 per cell; no repeated sampling within pairs).

### 3.3 Presentation

Each rater is shown a pair of responses to the same ethical dilemma with the
condition labels (N-CoT vs. Standard CoT) hidden. Responses are presented as
"Response A" and "Response B" with label assignment randomised per pair per
rater. Raters are blind to generator model identity.

Raters are asked to judge which response is preferable on three dimensions,
each rated on a 7-point Likert scale:
  1. Overall quality of reasoning about the ethical dilemma.
  2. Quality of stakeholder representation (does the response consider all
     relevant parties and their interests?).
  3. Calibration of uncertainty (does the response appropriately acknowledge
     what is unknown?).

Raters then indicate a global preference ("A is clearly better" / "A is
somewhat better" / "Roughly equal" / "B is somewhat better" / "B is clearly
better").

### 3.4 Rater pool

Raters are recruited via Prolific. Inclusion criteria:
- Graduate-level training or professional experience in at least one of: moral
  philosophy, clinical ethics, bioethics, law, policy.
- English as a primary or professional language.
- No prior participation in research using the DailyDilemmas corpus.

Each pair is rated by three independent raters. Majority rule determines the
pair-level verdict. Inter-rater agreement is reported as Cohen's kappa on the
global preference scale collapsed to three categories (A better / Tie / B better).

Minimum kappa target: 0.40 (moderate agreement). If the pilot batch (10 pairs
x 3 raters) falls below kappa=0.40, the screening criteria will be tightened
before the full run.

### 3.5 Blinding protocol

- Raters see only the scenario text and the two responses. No system-prompt
  information or model names are shown.
- Response order (A/B assignment) is randomised per rater using a seed derived
  from `SHA1(pair_id + rater_prolific_id)[:8]` to ensure reproducibility.
- The study coordinator who generates the presentation materials does not
  participate in data analysis.
- Analysis is conducted by a team member who did not participate in generating
  or labelling the stimuli.

## 4. Analysis Plan

### 4.1 Primary analysis

For each of the 50 pairs, the pair-level verdict (N-CoT preferred / Tie /
Standard CoT preferred) is coded as (+1 / 0 / -1). A one-sided Wilcoxon
signed-rank test is applied to the 50 signed preference scores. Significance
threshold: p < 0.05 (one-sided).

Effect size: matched-pairs rank-biserial correlation r.

### 4.2 Secondary analyses

- Within-pair Wilcoxon signed-rank on each of the three sub-dimension scales
  (stakeholder representation, uncertainty calibration, overall quality).
  Three tests, each at alpha=0.05 with no correction (exploratory).
- By-generator breakdown: proportion of N-CoT preferred / Tie / Standard CoT
  preferred per generator. No significance test at the generator level.
- Bootstrap CIs (N=2000, seed=42) on all effect sizes.
- Subgroup analysis by topic group (10 groups x 5 pairs each); directional
  report only, no significance test.

### 4.3 Exclusion criteria (pre-specified)

Rater responses are excluded from analysis if:
- Median response time < 45 seconds (likely did not read both responses).
- All 50 global preference ratings are identical (inattentive responding).
- Rater reports in the post-task survey that they recognised the system prompts
  or model identities.

## 5. IRB Plan

The study involves recruitment of human participants for a paid judgement task
via Prolific. The following IRB submission elements have been identified:

- **Risk level:** Minimal. Participants read ethical dilemmas and make
  comparative judgements; no sensitive personal data collected.
- **Compensation:** Market-rate Prolific payment (~$12/hour pro-rated to
  estimated 30-minute completion time for 50 pairs).
- **Data management:** Prolific IDs pseudonymised; no link to real-world
  identity retained beyond the Prolific platform. Data stored in an encrypted
  drive; deleted after publication.
- **Consent:** Prolific standard consent form plus a study-specific information
  sheet explaining the nature of the task and the blinding protocol.
- **Jurisdiction:** Submission to [Institution IRB Board -- to be specified
  prior to run]. Estimated review timeline: 4-6 weeks.
